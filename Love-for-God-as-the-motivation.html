Love for God as the Motivation

It can be easy to discount love as a good motivation because why would we want to help anyone else if it doesn't help us? When we follow a commandment, we might first think "God will bless me if I follow this commandment, so I will." But is that actually a motivator? "Charity never faileth", "... but endureth forever" -- that's a pretty big promise. Does understanding that we'll be blessed fail? We could call this "logic". Logic helps guide our love, but it is perhaps not the best motivator, because what if we actually don't want to be blessed? Logic didn't work for the adversary and those that followed him. They didn't have love for God or others -- including for themselves. Yet, Christ, when He said "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?", He might have well logically determined that the pain was not worth continuing enduring for, or that He could settle for a lesser reward. It might have been His love for His Father and for us that He continued enduring for. Maybe it was then that He proved that "charity" -- which is the "pure love of Christ" -- "never fails". When we face difficult circumstances in life and understand that we should want God's blessings but are having a hard time deciding if it's really worth it, maybe for us too it will be the charity that God gives us to helps us keep the commandments, despite any pain or laziness or fatigue motivating us otherwise.
