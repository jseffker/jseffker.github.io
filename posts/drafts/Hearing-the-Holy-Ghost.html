It's maybe not the specific actions themselves that God needs us to do in order to receive His blessings, but just the act of trying to follow Him. Adam and Eve lived in the Garden of Eden for who knows how long, with perfect bodies, and nothing could stop that until God took His blessing away from them and cursed the earth. Doctrine and Covenants also tells us that all commandments are spiritual and not temporal. To use the Word of Wisdom as an example, maybe it's not the specific things we do that keep us healthy, but God giving us the health that He determines we need as we follow Him (which, obeying the Word of Wisdom is a way to demonstrate our willingness to follow Him). When the early apostles woke Jesus during the storm because their ship was going to sink, He didn't start telling them a bunch of different things they should do or should've been doing regarding the ship itself, but to be not afraid and only believe. God is powerful enough to heal us despite our actions, because even in our best effort, our actions our often still very imperfect. But it's more our intent that matters, because if we act with intent, we are demonstrating our agency that we want to choose His way, along with all the blessings and hardships God will give us because of it. If we use our agency to show we want this choice, He will give it to us, and that's faith in Christ. We can do all good things through faith in Christ, and not anything without that faith, because God will not take away our agency. If we show God that we do not want to choose His blessings/hardships in our lives, He will not force them into our lives, because He won't take away our agency. If we act from a fear that we'll be missing something God is telling us, that's acting out of fear, and may likely be used by the adversary to get us to do things that are incorrect. If we trust that God has the power to make sure we are able to follow His voice, even if we don't always recognize it, and without having to constantly be in a state of second guessing, which is not healthy, then that's exercising faith in Christ. Also, sometimes we are too afraid of missing God's voice because we think that if we miss it, we'll suffer some pain that God won't be able to fix, or that we'll otherwise suffer pain. We'll always suffer pain, no matter what choices we make. Consider Christ's life. Was Christ's life perfect? No. He actually suffered greater pain than anyone ever has.
